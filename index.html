Samp, also known as "Umngqusho" in the Xhosa Culture, is a staple for many South African homes especially during the cold winter season. It is a very popular dish in the Xhosa Culture and is served as a main dish in many of their ceremonies. Some prefer it plain and some prefer it with beans (iimbotyi) to give it a bit of character. This dish is usually had on its own or with your choice of vegetables and meat. The people of the Xhosa culture tend to get very creative and enjoy it with amasi or milk and some with sheep head (Inyama yentloko).

In this recipe, we will be making a not so plain, plain gourmet samp served with a mouth-watering side of mutton stew.

Instructions

Prepping Samp:

  • Thoroughly rinse samp with lukewarm water for at least 2-3 minutes, removing any spoiled grains.

  • Soak samp in boiling hot water for 30-45 minutes. This is to soften the samp to reduce cooking time.

  • Once time has lapsed, drain the samp and rinse with cold water to remove any excess starch remaining.

Cooking Samp:


  1. Bring 4 Cups of water to a boil.


  2. Add 1 tablespoon salt and add 30ml cooking oil, to water.



  3. Once water starts boiling, add samp and stir using fork.


  4. Reduce heat to medium heat and let it cook (closed) for 3 hours, stirring occasionally.


  5. At 30 minutes to readiness, throw in chopped onion and potatoes.




  6. Add 1/2 tsp Black Pepper and 1 1/4 tbsp Aromat Seasoning to taste. *


  7. When potatoes have softened, add all your peppers and 2 tbsp butter. Mix together till the potatoes are mashed and peppers distributed evenly within the samp.




  8. Serve hot with a side of mutton stew.

* If water is not visible, add 1 cup of warm water.
